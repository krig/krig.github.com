Supernatural

I do not believe in the supernatural. That doesn't mean that it doesn't fascinate me. Ever since I was small, I've always loved to read about ghosts, telekinesis and life on other planets. Now, you might say that this doesn't make me very exceptional, in fact you might claim that most people wouldn't consider unicorns and goblins as anything real.

There is one form of belief in the supernatural, however, that most people accept without much questioning. That is, when it is presented as religion. You would think it quite odd of me to worship Mithra or Ba'al, but when someone thanks "god" for letting them win the lottery, that is just par for the course.

Why is this?

Why is religion accepted these days? Don't we know better? I mean, it's okay for someone who doesn't know about atoms and gravity and lasers to think that it follows from A) I make things, and B) there are things, that C) someone must have made them. That is not a level of reasoning that is acceptable for someone who has at least a rudimentary education.

The meaning of life

Here is how I see it: Objectively, there is no meaning. There is no great reason for things to be the way they are. The reason good people die and bad people get rich, that babies suffer and rapists triumph.. is not the obscure will of some malevolent sky-father. It's just the way things have turned out, for better and worse. Now, for me, this creates hope. I don't understand the people who can look at this world and the state of current affairs and think "God made this, how nice of him." Yeah, he was being real nice when he created poisonous snakes and parasites that swim up the urethra, and I especially like how he made that appendix of mine infect and burst at one point, something that would have killed me if it wasn't for satanic science coming to the rescue.

The reason all this gives me hope, however, is that since there is no god with some great plan and there is no layed out direction for things to take, I can change things. I can make things better.

Thus, as long as I live my life as well as I can, and try to do the right thing for those I care about, that's about all I can hope for. I don't have some naive expectation of the heavenly father recognizing my extraordinary level of faith and elevating me to some position of grandeur. This life is as good as it gets, and I try to live it as well as I can.
